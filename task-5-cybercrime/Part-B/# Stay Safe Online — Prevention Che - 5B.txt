## 🔒 General Internet Safety Tips

**Tip 1 — Use Strong and Unique Passwords**
Create passwords that are at least 12 characters 
long and include uppercase letters, lowercase 
letters, numbers and special symbols. Never use 
your name, date of birth or "password123" as 
your password. Use a different password for 
every account.

**Tip 2 — Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**
Always turn on 2FA on all important accounts 
including Gmail, Instagram, LinkedIn and GitHub. 
This adds an extra layer of security — even if 
someone steals your password, they cannot log 
in without the second verification code.

**Tip 3 — Never Click on Suspicious Links**
Do not click on unknown links received via 
WhatsApp, SMS, email or social media — even 
if they appear to come from a known contact. 
These links may install malware or steal your 
login credentials without your knowledge.

**Tip 4 — Keep Your Apps and Devices Updated**
Always install the latest software and security 
updates on your phone and laptop. Updates fix 
known security vulnerabilities that hackers 
actively exploit to gain access to your device.

**Tip 5 — Use Only Trusted and Secure Wi-Fi**
Avoid using public Wi-Fi at cafes, malls or 
railway stations for banking, shopping or 
logging into important accounts. Public Wi-Fi 
networks can be monitored by hackers to steal 
your data and login credentials.

**Tip 6 — Protect Your Personal Information Online**
Never share your Aadhaar number, PAN card 
details, college ID, home address or phone 
number publicly on social media or unknown 
websites. This information can be misused 
for identity theft and financial fraud.

## 💰 UPI and Financial Safety Tips
### (Specific to Indian College Students)

**Tip 7 — NEVER Enter Your UPI PIN to RECEIVE Money**
This is the most important UPI safety rule. 
You only need to enter your UPI PIN when you 
are SENDING money — never to receive it. If 
anyone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a 
QR code to receive a payment, it is 100% a 
fraud attempt. Reject and report immediately.

**Tip 8 — Never Share OTPs with Anyone — Ever**
Your OTP (One Time Password) is confidential 
and must never be shared with anyone — not 
even someone claiming to be from your bank, 
NPCI, Google Pay or PhonePe. Legitimate 
organisations NEVER ask for OTPs over 
call, chat or email under any circumstances.

## 📱 Social Media Safety Tips

**Tip 9 — Review Your Privacy Settings Regularly**
Check and update the privacy settings on all 
your social media accounts every few months. 
Make sure your posts, phone number and email 
address are visible only to people you trust 
and not to the general public.

**Tip 10 — Think Before You Post Anything Online**
Once something is posted online it can be 
screenshotted, shared and stored permanently 
— even after you delete it. Never post content 
that you would not want your professor, parent 
or future employer to see.

## 🚨 What To Do If You Are a Victim

**Step 1 — Act Immediately**
If you have been a victim of online fraud, 
call the National Cyber Crime Helpline 
immediately. Every minute counts as 
fraudsters move money quickly.

**Step 2 — Report Online**
File a complaint on the official National 
Cyber Crime Reporting Portal. Keep all 
screenshots, transaction IDs and chat 
records as evidence before reporting.

**Step 3 — Inform Your Bank**
Call your bank's customer care immediately 
to block your card and freeze suspicious 
transactions. Most banks have a 24x7 
fraud reporting helpline.
