## What is UPI/Online Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of 
cybercrime where fraudsters trick innocent users into 
transferring money or revealing sensitive financial 
details through deception, fake links, or social 
engineering. It is one of the fastest growing 
cybercrimes in India, targeting millions of smartphone 
users every year.

---

## How It Happens — Step by Step:

**Step 1 — The Setup**
The fraudster identifies a target, usually someone who 
has recently posted about buying or selling something 
online (e.g., OLX, Facebook Marketplace), or someone 
who has publicly asked for customer support help on 
social media.

**Step 2 — First Contact**
The fraudster contacts the victim pretending to be a 
buyer, seller, bank representative, or customer care 
executive. They sound polite, professional and 
completely convincing.

**Step 3 — The Trap**
The fraudster sends a fake UPI payment request or a 
malicious link saying "Click this to RECEIVE your 
payment." The victim, believing they are receiving 
money, enters their UPI PIN — which actually SENDS 
money instead.

**Step 4 — OTP Theft**
In some cases, the fraudster asks the victim to 
share their OTP "for verification purposes." Once 
the OTP is shared, the fraudster gains full access 
to the victim's bank account.

**Step 5 — Money Gone**
Within seconds, the entire amount is transferred 
to the fraudster's account. The money is then 
quickly moved across multiple accounts making 
it nearly impossible to trace or recover.

---

## Who Gets Targeted?

- First-time UPI users and elderly people
- College students buying/selling on OLX or 
  Facebook Marketplace
- People seeking refunds or customer support 
  on social media
- Small business owners and shopkeepers
- Anyone who publicly shares their phone 
  number online

---

## Real Case Scenario:

Ravi, a 19-year-old engineering student from Bhopal, 
listed his old laptop for sale on OLX for ₹15,000. 
A buyer contacted him and said he would pay via UPI. 
The buyer sent a UPI collect request of ₹15,000 
saying "accept this to receive payment." Ravi 
entered his PIN thinking he was accepting payment. 
Instead ₹15,000 was debited from his account. 
By the time Ravi realised the mistake, the 
fraudster had already disappeared.

---

## Consequences:

**For the Victim:**
- Immediate financial loss ranging from 
  hundreds to lakhs of rupees
- Emotional distress, anxiety and loss of trust
- Difficulty recovering money as transactions 
  are instant and hard to reverse
- Risk of identity theft if personal details 
  were shared

**For Society:**
- Erosion of trust in digital payment systems
- Increased fear of using UPI among new users
- Financial burden on families, especially 
  students and middle-class households
